Prologue – The Blood Seal

INTRAMUROS Manila, sixteen twenty one.
The convent burned like a candle offered to heaven.
Father Ignatius de Alonzo staggered through the smoke, cassock torn, a silver cross clutched in one trembling hand. The air smelled of salt, blood, and gunpowder — the scent of a world being unmade.

Behind him, the demon howled from its prison of iron and prayer. The Jesuits had trapped it beneath the foundations of Intramuros, a creature of famine and deceit that had devoured half a province before the Inquisition arrived. The others were dead; only Ignatius remained to finish the seal.

He dropped to his knees before the altar stone. Latin spilled from his cracked lips as he carved symbols into the marble with a knife made from a saint’s rib. When the final word of the rite was spoken, he pressed his palm to the wound on his chest and let his blood fill the grooves.

“Sanctus ignis, custodi hoc civitatem…”
Holy fire, guard this city.

The earth shook. The walls screamed. Light burst upward like dawn beneath the sea — and then silence.

The demon was gone, bound beneath Manila forever. But as Ignatius collapsed beside the dying flame, he whispered a warning that would echo through centuries:

“When steel rides and neon burns, the blood demon shall awaken.”

Chapter 1 – The Keys of the Monk

Neo-Manila, 2056.
Rain hissed against the neon streets like static.
Inside The Monk’s Keys, the world felt older — wood, brass, and the comforting smell of malt instead of ozone…

Brother John Michael soldered sparks between his gloved fingers. Padre Kaelen Valerio — “Father Chrome” — pulled on his black armor beneath a priest’s collar.

Marie Lu poured consecrated ale and threatened both of them with eternal dish-washing duty if they blew up her bar again.

And somewhere in the crimson night, something ancient began to remember its promise.
